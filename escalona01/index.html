Through Persecution, Political Asylee, Perseverance and Success

My journey

It was April 2010. I was on a flight from Caracas, the capital of an embattled Venezuela, to the United States. Like many coming to the United States, I was afraid: afraid of leaving my country, my culture, my family, and all that I held dear.

I faced many hardships in then-President Hugo Chavez’s Venezuela, subject to verbal and physical persecution for standing in public opposition to a government that had no respect for such universally accepted rights such as freedom of speech.

Defy distance. Interact and learn from industry experts, other top professionals and eXp Realty management. eXp Realty agents build teams, share best practices and enjoy a powerful referral network.

I knew I was heading from a daunting set of difficulties to a new frontier. While the sum of my experiences did not break my character, they shaped and changed my outlook on life and motivated my yearning for success and accomplishment. I welcomed the change approaching on the horizon.

venezuelan born american by choice

  • In June of 2011, I was granted political asylum in the United States. At that point, I resided in San Antonio, Texas for well over a year. Inspired by my own desire to better integrate into America’s social fabric, wanting to live somewhere where my English language skills would be tested, and buoyed by my newly-granted legal status, I decided to move to Pittsburgh in western Pennsylvania.

  • Upon arriving in Pittsburgh, I eagerly set out in search of new opportunities. I worked several different jobs, from production line in molding injections for medical devices in Latrobe PA; to working as a cashier and customer service associate at Home Depot in Greensburg, PA; but none felt like they challenged me or put the breadth of my skills to play. It wasn’t until I was given the opportunity to work at Gateway Health Plan in downtown Pittsburgh in January of 2015 as Bilingual Customer Service representative, that I truly started feeling like I was making a difference.

  • Gateway Health Plan is a managed care organization that holds contracts with the state governments’ Departments of Health in Pennsylvania, Delaware, West Virginia, and Arkansas, serving the needs of a mix of Medicare and Medicaid recipients in those states. In August 2017, I was promoted to Senior Customer Service Representative. In March 2019, I was promoted to Lead Customer Service Representative; same month I joined the CGS Student Government as Media Chair. I was recently promoted to Business Manager.

  • My successes, however, have not been without their struggles. To this day, communicating on the phone for this job has been a constant challenge, and I am often at the receiving end of verbal abuse, and discrimination from health plan members who are irate about their own struggles; and, yet, my passion and desire to make a difference in people’s lives, and my ambition to succeed in all that I do, have given me the strength and drive me to overcome even the most difficult of moments.

next step for...

Whether under the fist of Chavez’s authoritarian regime and its complete disregard for human rights; or in coming to a completely socially and culturally distinct country and leaving everything behind in pursuit of freedoms;

or in working to pursue a university degree while working full-time at Gateway Health (A Highmark Health affiliate): I have always welcomed challenges and the opportunities for growth they provide, always seeing tackling my personal growth as the conduit for what will enable me to fulfill my desire to help those in need.

Therefore, getting a degree from the University of Pittsburgh is the logical next step for my pursuit of excellence and perpetual self-betterment. Unlike when I first arrived in the United States as an immigrant full of fears and hopes with his eyes on the clouds, this time, with my feet firmly on the ground and with my eyes set on the University of Pittsburgh, I do not just welcome the changes on the horizon; I actively seek them.
